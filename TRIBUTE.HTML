Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

The Missile Man of India

About the Legend

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, known as the "Missile Man of India," was one of the most beloved presidents and scientists of India. He played a crucial role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. His simplicity, humility, and dedication made him a role model for millions.

Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, Dr. Kalam’s journey from a small-town boy to the President of India is truly inspiring. He always believed in the power of dreams and hard work, famously saying, "Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts and thoughts result in action."

Achievements

  • Developed India’s first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III).
  • 11th President of India (2002–2007).
  • Recipient of Bharat Ratna, India’s highest civilian honor.
